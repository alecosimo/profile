The classical impact laws of Newton and Poisson are able to correctly model the behaviour observed in single-contact collisions in most situations. However, this is not the case for problems characterized by multiple impacts. In this work, the nonsmooth generalized-α method is extended in order to correctly account for multiple impacts. The active set of the velocity level sub-problem is redefined in such a way that closed contacts with zero pre-impact velocity are considered inactive. Then, multiple impacts are solved as a sequence of impacts taking place within the same time step, instantaneously.
